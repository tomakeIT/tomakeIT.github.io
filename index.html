Research & Projects

Human Error Model & NeuralMetric

Collaborating with Mcity at the University of Michigan, I investigated the impact of human errors on traffic safety. This research led to the development of NeuralMetric, a machine learning framework that significantly improves collision prediction accuracy and computational efficiency. The work is currently under review.

X. Yan, H. Sun, J. Ni, H. Zhu, S. Feng, and H. Liu, "NeuralMetric: An Accurate and Efficient Real-time Safety Metric for Automated Driving Systems" Under Review at the 25th International Symposium on Transportation and Traffic Theory.

TeraSim 1.0: Autonomous Vehicle Simulation Platform

I contributed to TeraSim 1.0, a unified simulation platform that integrates realistic traffic scenarios with advanced 3D visualization using CARLA and Unreal Engine 5. This platform serves as a comprehensive testbed for autonomous vehicle research.

Ablation Study on Tri-Perspective View on Plane Configurations

I conducted a detailed study on 3D semantic occupancy prediction using the Panoptic nuScenes dataset, demonstrating that a two-plane configuration can outperform traditional methods in terms of accuracy.
